What is the OSOS Method?

When I got out of the service and started my photography career I used the same discipline acquired during basic training to understand this new craft. I devoured all the training I could, online and offline, but I still felt like something was missing.

All that changed when I stopped seeing my camera as a camera and started seeing it as a rifle. I began to stalk shots, control my breathing, realizing that every moment was a moment in time that needed to be captured before it was gone forever.

There is power in capturing a moment. It is a great honor to be a photographer. We have a responsibility to ensure the moments we capture are not just pictures.

The One Shot One Kill Method (OSOS) forces photographers to stalk their shots will military precision, anticipate moments before they occur and reduce the total number of shots taken. As a result, OSOS allows the shooter to reduce total editing time, increase the quality of final images and avoid the embarrassment of missing the perfect shot.

Since developing this method, I've built a successful career, being able to charge upwards of $1000 an hour for shots a normal photographer wouldn't be able to take.
